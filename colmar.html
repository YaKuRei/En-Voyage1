  • Fairytale town Colmar

    Last Christmas, my friends and I went to Colmar, a lovely town in the northeast of France. It has many narrow alley between candy-like houses. People call it little Venice in France. Compared with the true Venice, Colmar is more colorful.

  • Traffic

    It’s not very easy to get to Colmar. We started our trip from Paris, and spent about four hours to take the train to get there. Unfortunately, one of my friends and I got up late, so we missed our train and had to buy another ticket. However, we soon found that Colmar was worth the pay.

    It took about ten minutes’work to get into the old town from the train station. After meeting with others again, we couldn’t wait to explore the colorful houses.

  • Town scenery

    Spending a whole morning, we walked through the town. On the way, we saw lots of well-preserved 16th century architectural style buildings with pink and yellow Vosges sandstone and timber framing. All of those make the town full of fairytale style.

  • Little Venice

    The most beautiful scenery is at the canal. Standing by the sunlight, I can forget all the troubles.

  • Christmas market

    The market in Colmar also contains a variety of small markets. There are not only creative shops of artists, but also lively snack market on the square.

  • The biggest event is the annuel Christmas market, but there are some shops here even if it’s not during the Christmas. There’s not big shopping mall like in Paris. Most of the shops are lovely and exquisite.

  • You can also break into many cute candy shops, chocolate shops, bread workshops...

  • Howl's Moving Castle

    One of the most attractive things is that Colmar is the prototype of Hayao Miyazaki’s movie " Howl's Moving Castle ", which makes it a real colorful fairy tale world.

  • The recommended time to play is about two days. You can choose to book the accommodation in Airbnb to have a try live in the lovely pink Vosges sandstone house.
